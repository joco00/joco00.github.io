Lessons learned 2021

September 2021

**this is an unfinished post**

  • Its the moments where you have no control over and that seem miserable at the time that can end up being the most memorable. When I was in Joshua Tree I was camped out 2 miles into the desert. At night the tempuratue drop from 70 to 30 at night with the addition of 40 mph winds. I thought I was going to freeze to death, but I didn't!! I wrote a journal entry after i got back to my car and I cry laughinh everytime I read it.
    Lesson learned: These moments can be the most memorable and funny to look back on.

  • Karma. Good things happen to us and when we give good back to the world. The key is being observant and recongnizing when the good and bad happen.

  • The view is only as beautiful as the journey was. Climbing a mountain is hard. The view is breathtaking at the top, not only from its beauty but from the work you put in getting there. At the top no one is talking, no one is complaining about the bugs, no is doing anything. The top of mountains are still. The people who climb mountains understand this. I went on a hike to the top of a mountain in the Adirondacks with my girlfriend at the time. A mile in she had it. We turned around and drove to the top of a different mountain, payed $40 bucks to get in and had a horrible time. People were complaining about the short hike to the top thats was rocky and didnt have stairs, people were complaining about bug, people took photos and left. No one sat down and simply enjoyed what was infront of them.

  • Energy managment > time managment

  • Being alone in nature is not loney. Finding company in the world around you is an amazing feeling
